3 Typography System
✓ Verified from Live CSS

"Abridge Font" — Custom Proprietary Typeface

The H1 font-family declaration is "Abridge Font", sans-serif. This is the custom typeface developed by Pentagram (referred to in their case study as "Abridge Display"). It renders at 400 weight, uppercase, with 68px default size on desktop. The letterforms are architecturally inspired — the "A" has a flat top like a bridge arch, with open counters and a mix of geometric and humanist proportions. Each letter can split and recombine as part of a generative identity system. This typeface is proprietary and not publicly available.

Strategic Read for Your Rebrand

  • Single-accent discipline: Abridge uses exactly ONE non-neutral color across their entire brand. Enduring Orange does all the work — CTA buttons, decorative bridge graphics, link hover states. This extreme restraint makes the brand instantly recognizable. It's a bold choice that most competitors haven't matched.
  • Not actually dark-themed: Despite the Pentagram case study suggesting dark applications, the live site is white-first. The warmth comes from the Canvas (#F5F3EB) off-white and the orange accent, not from dark backgrounds. This is more approachable than a dark-first aesthetic.
  • Anti-healthcare palette: No blue, no green, no teal, no pastels. They've completely abandoned the conventional healthcare color language. This is rare in B2B healthcare AI and leaves traditional territory wide open.
  • Custom type as moat: "Abridge Font" is proprietary and cannot be replicated. The generative letterform system (where characters split into bridge shapes) creates a visual language that's uniquely theirs. Any competitor using a similar architectural type approach will look derivative.
  • Avantt as body font: Using a premium geometric sans (Avantt by FOMO Fonts) rather than a free Google Font signals investment in craft and premium positioning.
  • The bridge metaphor is fully owned: Name, logo, typeface, graphic system, and narrative are all built on the bridge concept. This territory is closed.
  • Potential vulnerability: The minimalist monochrome palette could feel cold or corporate. There may be space for a competitor that brings more warmth, color range, or visual energy while still feeling modern and trustworthy.
