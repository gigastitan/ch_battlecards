Typography System
Verified from Live CSS

Multi-Font System: Playfair Display + Space Grotesk + Inter

Capacity Health uses a distinctive serif + sans-serif pairing that differentiates from the geometric-sans-heavy competitor landscape. Playfair Display (serif) handles display headlines, creating an editorial, premium feel. Space Grotesk is used for the brand wordmark and navigation. Inter handles body/UI text and CTAs. Additional fonts loaded include DM Mono (monospace) and CameraPlainVariable (decorative, appears unused).

Display & Headlines — Playfair Display
The AI Operating System for High-Performance Acute Care.
Playfair Display is used for hero headlines and section headlines. Its high-contrast, transitional serif design gives Capacity Health an editorial, authoritative feel — a bold differentiation from competitors who rely on geometric sans-serifs like Sora, Euclid, or Helvetica Neue.

Strategic Self-Assessment — Design System Insights

  • Serif + sans pairing is distinctive. Playfair Display gives an editorial, premium feel that differentiates from the geometric-sans-heavy competitor landscape (Sora, Euclid, Helvetica Neue used by competitors). This is a genuine brand asset.
  • Single teal accent is clean but risks being forgettable. #0891A1 is professional but doesn’t command attention the way Qventus purple or Regard indigo does. Consider whether a bolder accent or secondary color is needed as the brand scales.
  • Warm cream backgrounds create approachability. The #F8F7F4 tone signals warmth and trustworthiness vs. the cold whites of most health-tech competitors. This is a subtle but meaningful differentiation.
  • Text-only wordmark needs evolution. As the brand matures beyond MVP, a distinctive logomark will be essential for recognition at small sizes (favicons, app icons, conference badges). This should be a near-term brand investment.
  • “Operating System” positioning is bold and ownable. Few competitors claim OS-level positioning. This creates clear differentiation from point solutions and signals platform-level ambition.
  • Stanford founder pedigree is a strong trust signal. The “built by practicing ER doctors” narrative is authentic and differentiating vs. pure-tech competitors. Both founders being active emergency physicians is a rare and powerful proof point.
  • The inverted CSS variable naming (--light = dark, --dark = white) should be cleaned up. This technical debt could cause confusion as the design system scales and new designers/developers onboard. Low effort, high impact fix.
